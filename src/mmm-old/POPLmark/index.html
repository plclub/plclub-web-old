The POPLmark Challenge

[The Problems] [Solutions] [Mechanized Metatheory for the Masses]

We issue here a set of challenge problems, dubbed the POPLmark Challenge, chosen to exercise many aspects of programming languages that are known to be difficult to formalize: variable binding at both term and type levels, syntactic forms with variable numbers of components (including binders), and proofs demanding complex induction principles. Our goal is to push the state of the art in formalizing programming language metatheory to the point where the technology is usable by the masses.

Overtime, we expect to post here proposed solutions to the challenge and discussions about those solutions. General tools and libraries that come out of the challenge will be posted at a companion site on mechanized metatheory for the masses. We may also post additional problems for the challenge when it becomes evident that the current set of problems are no longer challenging enough.

The Challenge Problems

Our challenge problems are taken from the basic metatheory of System F<:. This system is formed by enriching the types and terms of System F with a subtype relation, refining universal quantifiers to carry subtyping constraints, and adding records, record subtyping, and record patterns. The challenge problems are currently:

  1. Transitivity of the algorithmic subtyping relation
  2. Type safety
  3. Testing and animating with respect to the semantics

The official description, along with background information concerning the design of the challenge, is available for download: [PostScript] [Gzipped PostScript] [PDF]

The F<: interpreter for Challenege 3 is currently in need of work, but a preliminary implementation, written in OCaml, is available: [tar.gz].
